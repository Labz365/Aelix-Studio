Overview

A walk should be an experience, not a chore.

Wander starts from one input most walking apps ignore: time. Give it a starting point and how long you want to be out — it converts your pace into a distance and designs a route to match. Loops always return to your exact starting point, or you can choose a different finish and Wander sizes a scenic detour so you arrive right on schedule.

Intelligence

Interesting by design.

Before drawing a single line, Wander surveys the area around you using OpenStreetMap data: parks, waterways, landmarks, cafés and viewpoints you have asked it to find, and the things you have asked it to avoid — motorways, dual carriageways, industrial estates. It then compares several candidate routes and scores each on distance accuracy, scenery, road safety and novelty, so you get the best walk available rather than the first one it found.

Wander also remembers. Saved routes are kept on your device, and the generator actively steers new walks away from paths you have already taken — the same neighbourhood keeps producing new ground.

Navigation

Your maps app takes it from here.

Every generated route exports in one tap: open it in Google Maps with waypoints, hand the destination to Apple Maps, or download a GPX file that any navigation or fitness app can follow exactly. Wander plans the walk; the tools you already use guide it.

Privacy

Everything stays on your device.

Wander has no accounts, no tracking and no server of ours behind it. Your location is used only in your browser to plan the route, saved walks live in your device's local storage, and route calculations are made through the OpenStreetMap ecosystem's public services. Close the tab and Wander knows nothing about you.

Origin

Sparked by a friend.

Wander began as an idea from Kendra — that a walk shouldn't need a destination to be worth taking, just the time you're willing to give it. This one is hers as much as ours.
